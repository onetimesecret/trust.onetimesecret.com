Be clear about how our multi-tenant production domains are set up. This may need to go in terms, but noting it here so I don't forget.

The regional sites eu.onetimesecret.com are protected by CloudFlare orange cloud DNS proxy. Since September 2024 after prolonged DDoS attack on the site, interupted service continuity. We may revisit this choice from time to time but is currently the best of the art available.

Our onetime.co domain and subdomains do not use CloudFlare proxy so custom domains do not go through cloudflare's network. It is up to organization owner if they want to protect the site with cloudflare or any other similar proxy service. It can complicated the custom domain TLS certificate management and if you have problems contect support. Also mention that CloudFlare proxy performs TLS trermination as well (i.e. it's not unqie or specific to Approximated).

By default we use a third party approximated.app to request and manage TLS certificates. To do this, they need to use TLS termination on their inbound proxy nodes which aperates over multicast DNS (ed: I may have the name wrong, please correct me if wrong). The nature of multicast DNS is that the nodes operate across jruisdictions (ed: research approximated docs so we can be crystal clear about the language here). This is mentioned also in "There are two narrow exceptions...". We don't want to duplicate content so one section must be the surce of truth and the rest refer to it.

Depending on region and plan details, we may offer alternatives. Please contact support to inquire about the details.

For single tenant installs, we manage the TLS certificates on our infrastructure, on the singletenant instances or a combination. But no thirdparty services.
